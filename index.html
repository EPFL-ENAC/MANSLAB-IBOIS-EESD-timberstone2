Hybrid Timber-stone stuctures
Visual Abstract - Key results

  • How can we improve the application of such kind of construction technique?
  • How is the design of a hybrid timber-stone construction?
  • Could we automate the stacking of the stones?
  • How to make this type of construction more accessible to builders?
  • Is it possible to use scrub stone?
  • Could we use non transformed wood?

Historical context & research Question
There are several types of hybrid timber-stone construction around the world. The most impressive is the Kath kuni construction type of the northern parts of India. This regions is known for his earthquake frequencies. Nevertheless you can find 30 ff/meter high towers which are very old and survives several earthquakes.
Students Mockups
To explore different possibilities of designing a hybrid timber-stone construction we did an exercise with the student of the MANSLAB (Bachelor). The outcome are mockups in the scale 1/10. They explored the nature of the wood and the possibilities of prefabricated modules in the hope to improve the building process.

Building of the wall

To learn about the dry stone stacking technique we filmed the craftsmen building a wall of 1,5m length, 0,7m width and 1,5m height. The stone has been selected by the experimented builder in a stone quarry near the EPFL. A second wall has been built by the same craftsmen and the same wood, but with scrub stone of an other quarry.

The result of the second wall is not very convincing.
